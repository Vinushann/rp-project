# Weather Impact on Coffee Shop Sales — Negombo, Sri Lanka

## Negombo Climate Overview
- Negombo is a coastal city in the Western Province of Sri Lanka, approximately 35 km north of Colombo.
- Tropical climate with two main monsoon seasons.
- Average annual temperature: 27–30°C.
- Annual rainfall: approximately 2,300 mm.

## Monsoon Seasons
### Southwest Monsoon (May–September)
- Heaviest rainfall period for Negombo (Western coast).
- Average monthly rainfall: 200–350 mm.
- Foot traffic decreases 10–15% compared to dry months.
- Delivery orders increase by 20–30% as customers prefer staying indoors.
- Hot beverages sales increase by 15–25%.
- Cold beverage sales drop by 10–15%.
- Action: Shift marketing focus to delivery. Promote hot drinks and comfort food. Ensure delivery packaging is waterproof.

### Northeast Monsoon (December–February)
- Less rainfall in Negombo (primarily affects Eastern coast).
- Tourist season for Negombo — international tourists increase foot traffic.
- Cooler temperatures (25–28°C average).
- Action: Cater to tourist preferences. Offer international menu items. Extend hours.

### Inter-Monsoon Periods (March–April, October–November)
- Unpredictable rainfall. Thunderstorms possible.
- Generally warm and humid.
- Coffee shop traffic is moderate to high.
- Action: Maintain balanced inventory. Be prepared for sudden weather shifts.

## Temperature Effects on Product Sales

### Cool Days (< 26°C average)
- Hot beverages: +20–30% above average
- Hot food (soups, warm pastries): +15–20%
- Cold beverages: -10–15%
- Ice cream and cold desserts: -15–20%

### Warm Days (26–30°C average)
- Balanced demand across hot and cold items.
- Standard sales patterns apply.
- Comfortable dining weather increases dine-in traffic.

### Hot Days (> 30°C average)
- Cold beverages: +20–30% above average
- Iced coffee and smoothies: +25–35%
- Hot beverages: -10–15%
- Ice cream and cold desserts: +20–25%
- Foot traffic may decrease during peak heat (12–2 PM) but compensated by increased cold drink orders.

## Rainy Day Strategies
1. **Delivery push:** Run promotions on delivery platforms when rain is forecast.
2. **Comfort menu:** Feature hot chocolate, soups, warm pastries prominently.
3. **Social media alerts:** Post cozy cafe images during rainy weather to attract dine-in customers who want ambiance.
4. **Reduced perishable prep:** Lower food preparation volumes by 10–15% on rainy days to minimize waste.
5. **Staff adjustment:** Reduce front-of-house staff by 1 person on heavy rain days; maintain delivery prep staff.
