# Coffee Shop Operations Guide — Negombo, Sri Lanka

## Staffing Best Practices

### Peak Hour Staffing
- Morning rush (7:00 AM – 10:00 AM): Requires 3–4 staff members. Coffee orders dominate during this window, so barista coverage is critical.
- Lunch period (12:00 PM – 2:00 PM): Food orders peak. Kitchen staff should be at full capacity. Front-of-house needs 2–3 servers.
- Evening rush (5:00 PM – 7:00 PM): Mixed orders (beverages + snacks). 2–3 staff recommended.
- Off-peak (10:00 AM – 12:00 PM, 2:00 PM – 5:00 PM): 1–2 staff sufficient. Use this time for prep work, restocking, and cleaning.

### Weekend vs Weekday
- Weekends typically see 20–30% higher foot traffic than weekdays.
- Saturday is usually the busiest day of the week.
- Sunday afternoons have higher dine-in traffic as families visit.
- Weekday mornings are dominated by takeaway and delivery orders from office workers.

### Staffing During Holidays
- Pre-holiday days (1–2 days before a major holiday): Staff at 120% capacity. Customers stock up on items.
- Holiday day itself: Depends on the holiday. Poya days may see reduced foot traffic as many shops close, but cafes that stay open can capture increased demand.
- Post-holiday (1 day after): Slight dip in traffic, run with reduced staff.

## Inventory Management

### Perishable Items
- Order fresh ingredients (milk, cream, pastries, bread) daily or every other day.
- Maintain a 1.5-day buffer stock for perishables.
- Track daily wastage: If wastage exceeds 5% of daily stock, reduce next order proportionally.
- Use FIFO (First In, First Out) for all perishable storage.

### Non-Perishable Items
- Coffee beans, sugar, syrups, cups, and packaging: Reorder when stock falls below 1-week supply.
- Bulk purchase non-perishables monthly for cost savings.

### Monsoon Season Adjustments
- During monsoon (May–September in Negombo), foot traffic drops 10–15%.
- Reduce perishable orders by 10–15% to minimize waste.
- Increase stock of hot beverages and comfort food ingredients.
- Keep extra packaging for delivery orders (delivery demand rises when it rains).

## Order Type Optimization

### Delivery
- Delivery orders typically have higher average ticket value than dine-in orders.
- Ensure packaging maintains food quality (insulated bags for hot items, sealed containers for cold drinks).
- Partner with delivery platforms and optimize menu for travel-friendly items.
- Delivery spikes during rainy weather — prepare extra packaging and ingredients.

### Takeaway
- Takeaway dominates morning hours (office workers, commuters).
- Speed is critical — aim for under 3 minutes per order.
- Offer loyalty cards or stamp programs to retain repeat takeaway customers.

### Dine-In
- Dine-in customers spend more time and often add desserts or second beverages.
- Weekend dine-in traffic is highest during afternoon hours.
- Maintain ambiance: clean tables, comfortable seating, good music.
- Offer combo deals for dine-in to increase average order value.
