# Product Catalog and Category Guide

## Product Categories

### Hot Beverages
- Includes: Classic Coffee, Cappuccino, Latte, Espresso, Hot Chocolate, Chai Tea, Green Tea
- Peak season: Monsoon months (May–September) and cooler months (December–February)
- Temperature correlation: Sales increase by 15–25% when daily average temperature drops below 26°C
- Best-selling times: Morning (7–10 AM) and evening (5–7 PM)

### Cold Beverages
- Includes: Iced Coffee, Iced Latte, Smoothies, Fresh Juices, Milkshakes, Iced Tea
- Peak season: Hot dry months (March–April, August–September)
- Temperature correlation: Sales increase by 20–30% when daily average temperature exceeds 30°C
- Best-selling times: Midday (11 AM – 3 PM)

### Bakery & Pastries
- Includes: Croissants, Muffins, Danish, Cookies, Brownies, Cakes
- Consistent demand throughout the year with slight peaks during holidays
- Best paired with hot beverages for combo deals
- Morning rush sees highest pastry sales

### Main Food Items
- Includes: Burgers (Spicy Burger, Classic Burger), Pizzas (Deluxe Pizza), Pasta (Sweet Pasta, Crispy Pasta), Sandwiches, Wraps
- Peak times: Lunch (12–2 PM) and dinner (6–8 PM)
- Delivery-friendly items: Burgers, pizzas, and pasta travel well
- Highest margin items in the menu

### Desserts
- Includes: Deluxe Dessert, Crispy Dessert, Ice Cream, Puddings
- Sales spike during holidays and weekends
- Often ordered as add-ons to main meals (upselling opportunity)
- Afternoon and post-dinner are peak dessert times

## Complementary Product Pairings
- Hot Coffee + Croissant/Muffin: Classic morning combo, 35% of morning orders include both
- Burger + Cold Beverage: Popular lunch combo, especially during hot weather
- Pizza + Smoothie: Growing trend among younger customers
- Dessert + Hot Chocolate: Evening indulgence combo, popular on weekends
- Pasta + Fresh Juice: Healthy lunch option, popular with office workers

## Seasonal Menu Strategy
- **January–February (Cool season):** Promote hot beverages, soups, warm pastries
- **March–April (Hot dry season):** Launch iced specials, fruit smoothies, cold desserts
- **May–September (Monsoon):** Comfort food focus — hot drinks, pasta, pizza, warm desserts. Delivery promotions since foot traffic drops.
- **October–November (Inter-monsoon):** Balanced menu, prepare for holiday season
- **December (Holiday season):** Special holiday drinks, festive desserts, gift sets, catering packages

## Pricing Strategy
- Morning rush items should be competitively priced to capture commuter market
- Combo deals increase average order value by 25–40%
- Happy hour pricing (2–5 PM off-peak) can boost slow-period traffic by 15–20%
- Holiday specials can command a 10–15% premium
- Delivery orders should include a small markup (5–8%) to cover packaging costs
