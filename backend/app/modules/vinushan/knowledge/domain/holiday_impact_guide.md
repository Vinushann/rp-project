# Sri Lankan Holiday Impact Guide for Coffee Shops

## Major Sri Lankan Holidays and Their Business Impact

### Poya Days (Monthly Full Moon Holidays)
- Occur once every month (12 per year). Liquor sales are banned, but coffee shops benefit.
- Impact: +10–20% increase in cafe traffic as people look for alcohol-free social venues.
- Pre-Poya (1 day before): Slight increase in takeaway orders.
- Poya day itself: Dine-in traffic increases significantly as families and groups visit cafes.
- Post-Poya (1 day after): Return to normal levels.
- Action: Stock extra desserts and beverages. Extend operating hours if possible.

### Sinhala and Tamil New Year (April 13–14)
- The biggest cultural celebration in Sri Lanka.
- Pre-New Year (April 10–12): Sales spike as people prepare celebrations. Gifting and catering orders increase.
- New Year days (April 13–14): Many shops close, but those that stay open see 30–50% higher traffic from travelers and tourists.
- Post-New Year (April 15–17): Gradual return to normal. Some leftover festive demand.
- Action: Create special New Year menu items, offer catering packages, extend hours during peak days.

### Vesak (May — Full Moon day)
- Celebrates the birth, enlightenment, and death of Buddha. Major national celebration.
- Pre-Vesak (2–3 days before): Decorations go up across the country. Light food demand increases.
- Vesak day and day after: Many businesses close. Cafes that operate can capture significant demand.
- Vesak zone events (dansala — free food stalls): May reduce cafe foot traffic in some areas, but premium cafes are less affected.
- Action: Offer special Vesak-themed beverages (e.g., herbal teas, traditional drinks). Reduce perishable orders slightly.

### Christmas and New Year (December 25 – January 1)
- Major commercial period. Tourism peaks in Negombo (beach city near airport).
- Pre-Christmas (December 20–24): Strongest sales period of the year. Gift purchases, office parties, family gatherings.
- Christmas Day (December 25): Moderate traffic, tourists and families dining out.
- December 26–30: High tourist traffic in Negombo specifically.
- New Year's Eve and Day: Party orders, late-night service demand.
- Action: Staff at maximum capacity. Stock premium items. Enable late-night service. Create holiday specials and gift sets.

### Independence Day (February 4)
- National holiday with moderate commercial impact.
- Some increase in family outings and dine-in traffic.
- Action: Normal staffing with slight increase. Feature patriotic-themed specials if appropriate.

### Thai Pongal (January 14–15)
- Hindu harvest festival.
- Moderate impact in areas with Tamil population.
- Traditional sweets and treats are in demand.
- Action: Offer traditional-inspired desserts or beverages.

## General Holiday Patterns
- Pre-holiday periods (1–2 days before) consistently show 15–25% higher sales.
- Holiday days themselves vary — some holidays increase foot traffic, others reduce it.
- Post-holiday days typically see a 5–10% dip as people return to routine.
- Long weekends (holiday + weekend) create the strongest sales periods.
- Multiple consecutive holidays (e.g., New Year week) create sustained demand.

## Promotional Strategies for Holidays
- Launch holiday-specific menu items 3–5 days before major holidays.
- Offer combo deals during pre-holiday periods to maximize basket size.
- Create social media campaigns 1 week before major holidays.
- Partner with local events for brand visibility during festivals.
- Offer loyalty program double points during holiday periods.
